The International Space Station

These are some of the facts that I find particularly interesting, but this list is not meant to be comprehensive in any way. You can read more about the ISS on the Wikipedia page or on the NASA website.

  • The ISS orbits this earth at an average altitude of 250 miles.
  • It takes just over 1.5 hours for the ISS to circle the globe!
  • A coalition of agencies from the USA, Russia, Japan, Europe, and Canada support the ISS.
  • From end to end the ISS is as large as footballfield, although a large part of its structure is the solar array used to power the station.
  • The first piece of the current ISS structure went into orbit in 1998, and the station has been continually manned since November of 2000.
